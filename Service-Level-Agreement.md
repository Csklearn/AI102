Purpose
=======

This Service Level Agreement (SLA) defines the level of service expected from the Resilience Design System (RDS) team in supporting and maintaining the Resilience Design System, ensuring consistency, accessibility, and usability across digital products.

This SLA is agreed upon by the RDS team and its stakeholders to ensure mutual understanding and accountability.

Scope of Services
=================

The RDS team provides:
* Design system governance and evolution
* Component development and maintenance
* Documentation and usage guidelines
* Accessibility compliance
* Support for consuming teams (designers, developers)
* Versioning and release management

Response Times
==============

Defects
-------

We are committed to triaging and responding to reported defects such as bugs, accessibility issues, and documentation errors **within 2 business days**.

If we need more information to understand and identify the issue, then we will seek it from the reporter. Otherwise, we will respond to the reporter with a plan and a timeline for the fix.

If a defect is thought to be critical, then it should be raised to the **RDS Product Owner**, who will determine the criticality and help expedite if needed.

Design Updates & Feature Requests
---------------------------------

Requests for style changes and features including new components are handled and considered on a case-by-case basis. Changes related to the design or the experience should be submitted by a member of the Experience Design team.

Escalation Process
==================

If SLA targets are not met, escalate to the **RDS Product Owner** or whoever is covering in the absence of the PO. Any available RDS team member can assist you with reaching the acting PO.
